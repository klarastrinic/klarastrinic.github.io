12. Homicide by Logic feat. Eminem

Yes, there is an extensive, originally one-sided history between the two.

11. Middle Child by J. Cole

On the track, Cole envisions himself in the center of the old
and new generations of hip-hop, therefore making him the “middle child” of rap.
“Middle Child Syndrome” is a psychological condition
in which middle children sometimes feel
neglected by their families due to the attention
that their younger and older siblings receive.

9. Circles by Post Malone
“Circles” is the fourth single from Post Malone’s third studio album,
Hollywood’s Bleeding. The track continues the heartbreak theme from “Goodbyes”
as he struggles to maintain a strong relationship with this girl. Like “Goodbyes,”
“Circles” fell victim to leaks before official announcements.
A snippet of the track originally surfaced online in July of 2019, with the full
song being leaked online later in August.

7. West Coast by G-Eazy feat. Blueface (Remix)

This song is G-Eazy, Blueface, ALLBLACK and YG ode to the West
Coast of the U.S.; the 4 grew up in Oakland and Los Angeles, respectively.
Throughout the song plays a pitched-up vocal sample of “Let’s Ride” by Bay Area
legend Richie Rich. Additionally,
the music video features cameos from fellow West Coast musicians such as E-40,
Ty Dolla $ign, and Richie Rich himself.



6. EARFQUAKE

In an interview with Rick Rubin on his podcast Broken Record, Tyler explains that his inspiration
for the title came from there being an actual earthquake when he was writing the song.
Then he just decided to change the “th” to an “f.”
During Tyler’s concert hosted by Apple Music with IGOR presentation,
Tyler said the song is from 2017 and he wrote it for Justin Bieber,
but “he didn’t take it.” It was also offered to Rihanna but she also declined.


5. Follow God by Kanye West

 Kanye West revisits the spiritual themes of his February 2016 track,
“Father Stretch My Hands, Pt. 1,” on its 2019 successor, “Follow God.”
On the soulful track, Kanye flips a sample of Whole Truth’s 1974 track,
“Can You Lose By Following God” to discuss the way he struggles to live a religious
life and follow God’s direction in face of the difficulties that God presents.

3. Goodbyes by Post Malone

Throughout the song, Post sings about troubles with a girl and expresses his
desire to remove her from his life, while Thug’s part seems to
suggest that he’s trying to reconnect with a girl,
which is in complete contrast to the rest of the song.
A snippet of the song originally surfaced online on March 12, 2019,
and the full song leaked on March 14, 2019. After speculation by various
media outlets, Post confirmed the track would be released on July 5, 2019, in a Twitter post.


2. Devil's Work by Joyner Luucas
After more than 6 months since he announced his new album ADHD
and releasing the lead single for it titled
“I Love,” Joyner comes back with a second single, “Devil’s Work.”
Influenced by recent tragic deaths of rappers such as XXXTENTACION
and Nipsey Hussle, Joyner pours out his heart in this song that serves
as message to God, filled with many queries which question God’s
motives and reasoning, with the main one being
“Why do you take good people from us and leave the bad ones?”


1. The Ringer by Eminem

“The Ringer” sees Eminem continuing his criticism of the state of hip-hop in 2018,
focusing especially on mumble rappers, some of whom he calls out by name like Lil Xan and Lil Pump.
The song also continues themes he explored on 2018’s “Chloraseptic
(Remix).” Specifically, Eminem criticizes the double standards of the public
towards him in the wake of his 9th album, Revival. Critics are rewarded for pushing negative
views about him, but should he retaliate he falls further in their esteem.
Finally, Em also takes aim at Donald Trump again, pointing out
that the President has yet to answer his 2017 BET Hip-Hop Awards Cypher
Verse as well as several other missives he sent on Revival.
